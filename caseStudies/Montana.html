3.1 RWD Website: Montana State Parks


MISSION: Redesign a RWD website prototype for a Government Agency - Montana State Parks

PROBLEM: Montana residents and visitors need a reliable source to help them plan their next outdoor activity. The problem is their current state parks website is dull, confusing, and has bad interface. As a result, many users do not find their website to be helpful and user-friendly. With the help with one other Designer, we will redesign and restructure both the desktop + mobile interfaces to make it easier for users to navigate, and achieve their end goals painlessly.

TIMELINE: 3 weeks

ROLE: UX Researcher, UI Designer, Project Manager

TOOLS: Miro, Figma, Trello, Google Suite, ZOOM, Slack, Adobe Illustrator

PREFACE


What is MSPs?

Montana State Parks (MSPs) is a government agenecy whose mission is to promotes healthy, active, and environmentally aware communities by enhancing park land, enriching educational opportunities, increasing volunteerism, and improving community outreach in state parks.

3. USUABILITY TESTING


The purpose of this test is to identify key pain points from the users point of view. We have asked 5 users to action the following tasks + thoughts on Montana State Parks current website:

1. What is the address and Fee Information for Salmon Lake Park?
2. Which parks are recommended for learning and school field trips?
3. How many parks are there for Skiing?


After conducting all of our interviews, we begin to organize our data and ideas by doing a Affinity Diagram and Priorization Matrix






Key Takeaways:




4. IDEATE


After building our Affinity Diagram, we now have an idea of who our target audiences are. This will then helps us create our User Persona. Elizabeth Dirks, who is a wife and mother, lives in Montana, and looking to do more outdoor activities with her family.





Style Tile: Since the theme here is outdoors and adventurous, we knew we wanted to go wtih a green, and blue theme. We kept the logo because we felt this was their original asset, which cannot be replaced.





5. WIREFRAMES



1. During the research phrase, we discovered that most users visit MSPs to get information on a park, or to find an activity. Therefore, we wanted to display these two features on the home page as a call to action buttons.
2. We took out the side menu, and consolidated everything to the top dropdown navigation.
3. We also wanted to add an area displaying all the beautiful landmarks of Montana - a 2x6 matrix grid.
